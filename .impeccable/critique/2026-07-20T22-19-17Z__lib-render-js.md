## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No — it reads as authored.**

**LLM assessment:** Evidence of real intent dominates: French typographic micro-rules (NNBSP before `; : ! ?`, guillemets) that nobody adds by accident; an editorial lead-selection heuristic that encodes an opinion (multi-source coverage beats recency); a page-weight boast computed from actual rendered bytes; maintenance decisions with rationale in comments. Three tells remain: boilerplate "why it matters" analysis pasted onto a local-colour story, a decorative URGENT badge firing on trivia, and a truncated feed excerpt presented under the label "L'ESSENTIEL".

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` exit 2 — exactly **one rule** fired: `side-tab` ×4 (home/story/subscribe/brief, all line 112). All four are the *same* inlined CSS rule duplicated across pages: `.gist{border-left:3px solid var(--accent)...}` (`lib/render.js:187`). **Assessed as a false positive:** `.gist` is a pull-quote, not a card; a left rule on a pull-quote is a *filet*, centuries-old print convention, and the rule targets thick accent bars on shadowed/rounded cards. Zero other rules fired across all four pages. Notably the image-free architecture produced no false positives at all.

**The detector being clean is itself the finding:** the *aesthetics* are not sloppy. Every real problem below is behavioural, editorial, or in a mode the scanner didn't measure.

**Browser evidence (measured live):** No console errors. No horizontal overflow at 375px (scrollWidth 375 = clientWidth 375, zero elements past the edge). Light-mode contrast passes comfortably — body ink on paper far above AA, `--ink-2` secondary text at **6.69:1**. Bottom-nav tap targets **64×45px** — 3px under the 48px minimum, with 183px of unused bar width available.

## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely designed newspaper with three failures that undercut it, and they are not design failures — they are **trust failures**.

The identity works. The first 400px earn attention in under a second, the monogram system solves the no-images constraint elegantly, and the restraint is real (a 4/4 on minimalism is rare and earned). The craft pass landed.

But the site currently **tells a subscriber "✅ C'est fait !" and then never contacts them**, hides its own navigation below the footer on desktop, and renders its primary button at 2.7:1 contrast in dark mode. The biggest opportunity is not more polish — it is making the product's promises true.

## What's Working

1. **The colour system is a real system, not eight hand-picked values.** `hsl(var(--h) var(--sat) var(--lit))` drives all eight section hues from one formula, and every one clears AA (measured: CTA 6.49:1, URGENT 6.69:1, meta 6.67:1, SPORT kicker 5.62:1 in light mode). Changing the ramp changes all sections coherently.

2. **The monogram tile solves the image problem without looking like a fallback.** `hue()` hashes the source name to a stable colour; Maliactu reads warm brown, Al Jazeera cool blue, Financial Afrik rose — recognisable at a glance, deterministic, zero bytes. The Charter serif glyph makes it read as a masthead, not a placeholder.

3. **Showing today's actual Brief inline instead of a blind signup box.** Three real numbered headlines with sources sit above the CTA. "Here is the product" beats "sign up for the product" — the highest-leverage conversion decision on the site is already right.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] The subscribe form silently misfiles every non-Telegram subscriber.**
- **Verified in source:** `lib/render.js:416` posts `{channel:'telegram', ...}` unconditionally, while the field invites "Votre e-mail, pseudo ou numéro Telegram". The UI then replies "✅ C'est fait ! Vous recevrez les alertes."
- **Why it matters:** Someone who enters an email gets an unambiguous success message and will never receive anything. This is a broken promise at the exact moment the user extends trust — and Phase 2's entire success metric is subscribers. You would be counting people you cannot reach.
- **Fix:** Explicit channel choice (two radio pills: E-mail / Telegram), pass the chosen channel, set `type="email" inputmode="email" autocomplete="email" autocapitalize="none"` on the email variant, add `id`/`for` to both label-input pairs (currently zero `for=` attributes in the file). If email delivery isn't wired, say so rather than confirming success.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

**[P1] Dark mode fails contrast on the two most important elements.**
- **Verified in source:** `.btn{...color:#fff}` (line 163) and `.urgent{...color:#fff}` (line 115) never flip, but dark mode remaps `--accent` to `#4fae7e` and `--urgent` to `#ff6b71` (line 90). Measured live: **CTA 2.73:1, URGENT badge 2.77:1** — both fail AA (4.5:1) and even the 3:1 large-text floor.
- **Why it matters:** The primary conversion control and the highest-urgency signal are the two least legible things on the page — for the audience (18–35) most likely to have dark mode on by default.
- **Fix:** In `[data-th=dark]` add `--on-accent:#0d1f16; --on-urgent:#2b0a0c`; change `.btn{color:var(--on-accent)}` / `.urgent{color:var(--on-urgent)}`, keeping `#fff` in light. Dark text on light-tinted accents clears 8:1+ and reads more editorial, not less.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable audit`

**[P1] Desktop has no navigation.**
- **Verified in source:** `@media(min-width:720px){nav.tabs{position:static}}` (line 191). Since `nav.tabs` sits last in the DOM, it renders at offsetTop 5188 of a 5303px document — **below the footer**. Le Brief and Alertes are unreachable on desktop without scrolling the entire river.
- **Why it matters:** Diaspora readers skew desktop, and they are the segment most likely to subscribe and to pay later.
- **Fix:** At ≥720px move the three tabs into `header.top` beside the brand (already a flex row with room), and drop `body{padding-bottom:76px}` in the same query — currently 76px of dead space on every desktop page.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable adapt`

**[P2] URGENT and L'ESSENTIEL fire on nothing, devaluing both.**
- URGENT on a payment-receipts explainer and a bus parade; L'ESSENTIEL on the fragment "Lamine Yamal a été vu lors de".
- **Why it matters:** Badges that cry wolf retrain the reader to ignore the design system's loudest signals permanently.
- **Fix:** Suppress `.urgent` unless `c.breaking && c.others.length >= 2` (real events get multi-sourced fast). Suppress `.gist` unless the excerpt is ≥80 chars and ends on sentence punctuation — an absent gist reads as restraint, a truncated one reads as broken.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

**[P2] "Le Brief" names three different products.**
- Tab ✉ Le Brief → `/brief` (*Le Point Afrique de l'Ouest*, weekly). Homepage box → "Le Brief — chaque matin à 7 h" (daily). Tab 🔔 Alertes → `/subscribe`, whose H1 is *also* "Le Brief". And `/subscribe` says "chaque matin" while its trust line says "deux envois par jour maximum."
- **Fix:** Rename the weekly tab to **Le Point** (its actual masthead), reserve **Le Brief** for the daily, retitle `/subscribe` H1 to "Recevoir Le Brief", pick one frequency claim.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (one thumb, distracted, 3G):**
- `.pill` language/theme toggles measure **43×32 and 38×30px** — both under 44px, top-right, worst thumb zone. Bottom nav is 64×45px, 3px under minimum.
- Contact input is `type="text"` — iOS serves a text keyboard and capitalises "Diallo.badara@gmail.com". Nothing validates it.
- Submit has no pending state; on 3G she taps twice and gets no feedback either time.
- "Lire sur Maliactu →" has no `target`, so she leaves the site entirely; returning re-fetches the homepage and loses her scroll position 5,000px down, with no read-state to find her place.
- 7.8 screens of river with no "load more", no top-jump, no exit.

**Jordan (first-timer, never used an aggregator):**
- Nothing states what this is. The only explanation of the aggregator model ("chaque article renvoie vers sa source") is in the footer, 6,300px down — he reaches the outlink button never having been told the article lives elsewhere.
- "aussi sur Bamada.net" is unexplained jargon.
- He taps ✉ Le Brief expecting the daily thing the green box described, lands on a weekly with a different name, and finds *another* box offering "Le Brief" at the bottom.
- Tab says "Alertes", H1 says "Le Brief", button says "M'abonner aux alertes" — three labels, one subscription.
- `.item:hover h3{text-decoration:underline}` is the only clickability signal, and touch never triggers hover.
- `<label>` has no `for`, `<input>` has no `id` (confirmed: zero `for=` in the file) — tapping the label doesn't focus, and screen readers announce an unlabelled box.

## Minor Observations

- Inside a labelled band every row repeats the band's own kicker — MALI appears five times in one screen. Suppress the per-item kicker when it matches the band.
- Two Hydrocarbures stories (Maliactu 1h / Malijet 2h) sit adjacent, unclustered — the core promise visibly failing in front of the user.
- `.urgent{animation:pulse 1.6s infinite}` blinks forever (WCAG 2.2.2). `prefers-reduced-motion` covers most of it; consider capping iterations.
- The `/a/:id` back link reuses `.meta` at 12.48px — the only escape hatch on the page is its smallest text.
- `rel="noopener"` without `target` is inert. Either add `target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"` plus an external-link glyph, or drop it.
- The `@keyframes rise` comment claims content is visible by default, but `animation-fill-mode: backwards` holds it at `opacity:0` until the delay elapses. Harmless (CSS is inline) but the comment will mislead the next maintainer.
- No skip link; keyboard users tab ~29 headline links before reaching nav.
- `aria-label="theme"` conveys no state; `aria-pressed` + a real label costs ~40 bytes.

## Questions to Consider

1. **If URGENT and L'ESSENTIEL were deleted tomorrow, would anything of value be lost?** They currently fire on bus parades and sentence fragments. A system that only ships signals it can back is smaller *and* more trustworthy — and on a 20KB budget, smaller is the second prize.
2. **Is the river the product, or is the Brief the product?** The homepage spends 6,329px and 29 items building an infinite-scroll habit, then interrupts itself at item 7 to sell a 2-minute digest. Those are opposite behaviours. If the Brief is the business, why does the river outweigh it 20:1 by pixel?
3. **What does "conçue pour les connexions maliennes" mean once the reader taps a headline?** The page proudly reports ~21KB, then hands the user to Maliactu — likely 2–4MB with ad tech. Is the honest next move a reading mode that keeps them here, or dropping the byte-count boast?
